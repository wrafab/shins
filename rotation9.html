Rotation 9

Arduino and Raspberry Pi

For rotation 9, I had the opportunity to use Scratch and Python, the Raspberry Pi,various Hats,sensors and electronics to form the basis for our physical computing.

Raspberry Pi Project

This is my finished product!

My favorite part of the project was when I connected the Raspberry Pi to the computer and programmed the breadbox to light up in many different colors and even project what I wrote on the computer. As you can see above in the picture, I had my last name to be projected on the breadbox. It was interesting to see how the words that I typed on the computer came live. Also, setting specific time intervals to flash the light was interesting too.


What did I learn?

My experience using the Raspberry Pi was full of new challenges and excitement. When I first saw the motherboard, I was confused by what went where and what each parts did to the computer. However, when I began to learn about physical computing, things started to connect and utilizing Raspberry Pi wasn't that hard. Regarding physcial computing, I have learned that physical computing is a combination of software coding and hardware (computers, Arduino, Raspberry Pi) to sense and respond to the digital world. Physical computing requires extensive hands on approach, which makes it more appealing than other computing systems. Scratch and Python are important when using Raspberry Pi because they are based on the GPIO(General Purpose Input/Output), so they are compatible with Raspberry Pi .When the LED lights and my name appeared on the motherboard, I was impressed by the fact that even me, who did not have any background knowledge of the system, was able to create algorithms that generates my name and sends specific signals.
