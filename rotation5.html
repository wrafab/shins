Design rules and Limitations of 3D printer

There are some rules and limitations to follow when printing objects from the 3D printer. The first limitation is that the object you are intending to print should not be too small or too big. There is a set dimension for the 3D printer, and if the object is too big, it will not print the entire object. On the other hand, if the object is too small, there will be a lack of precision and accuracy. Another limitation of the 3D printer is that your model cannot have more than one material if you are using the MakerBot Replicator. For each printing process, the 3D printer only uses one material, thus a combination of other materials are impossible. Lastly, most additive manufacturing machine can't build a model without layers. Consequently, your finished product will inevitably have layers on it, and it is recommended to soften the surface after printing it.
