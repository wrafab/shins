Rotation 10

Final Project and Presentations

For the last rotation of the year, I had to utilize at least four different pieces of equipment and/or significantly different process to come up with an original final project.

Christmas Theme Box

For my Final Project, I am planning on making a Christmas theme box using CAD Design, Vinyl Cutting, Lazer Cut/Engraving, and 3D-Printing. This is my finished product!

For the materials, I have used wood, sticker vinyls, plastic, and glue gun. The longest part of my project was 3-D printing the christmas trees which took about 6~7 hours. Laser cutting took me about 15 minutes and printing out the stickers took me about 10 minutes. When I was laser cutting the wood, I had to make sure that the vector was set in hairline and raster was in 0.5. For my 3-D printing model, I had to make sure that the file was in makerbot form. Throughout working on my final project, the process was more about remembering what I have done so far rather than trying to learn something new. However, I did learn that combining digital components with existing objects required a lot of creativity and originality.


What did I learn?

It was great to use all the different techniques and skills that I have learned so far, and integrate them so that I can come up with one creation that combines all the key coomponents of the lessons that I've learned in this class. I am thankful for my teacher Mr.Gerber, my classmates, and all the Wang Innovation Center crew that helped me throughout this year and I would like to say that I have learned so much in this short period of time and will be stopping by the Wang Innovation Center periodically to keep up with my work. Thanks!
