Rotation 6

Vinyl Stickers/Molding/Urethane Coaster

My objective of rotation 6 was to design 3 black&white stickers and two multi colored stickers. Also, we had the opportunity to mill a wax using subtractive skills and create a coaster using urethane and expoxy.

Designing the sticker

This is my finished product!

Printing out colored stickers and the black/white stickers required me to use 2 different techniques. First, to print a black/white sticker, I had to use the Roland GS-24 Camm 1 vinyl cutter. I had created my outline for stickers on Corel Draw. I decided to creat a sticker of a Yin-Yang symbol, a diamond, and a Supreme sticker. I chose the Yin-Yang because the symbol is famous for only having two colors (black and white). I chose the diamond logo and the supreme logo because they were my 2 favorite skateboard/casual streetwear companies. On the other hand, in order to print colored stickers, I had to use the Roland 540i. Again, I had to outline my stickers on Corel draw, but I also had to make sure that I had an outline so that I can cut it out. For the colored stickers, I have printed the University of Notre Dame and North Carolina because they were my 2 favorite college teams. The biggest challenge for me was making sure that the wheels were on the spot so that the stickers won't move while printing. By going through all the process, I realized how much work was required for printing small stickers. However, now that I know how to use the machines, I look forward to printing more stickers so that I can decorate my desktop.


Molding From A Negative

This is my finished product!

Another task for rotation 6 was to form a chocolate mold using Fusion 360. I decided to put a Maseratti logo for my mold because it was my favorite car company and because it is my dream car. I have followed a video that taught me through how to make the logo on Fusion 360. It was important that the file was in .stl file. To print the mold, I used the MDX40A and Smooth-Sil 960. Unfortunatley, when the mold was finished, I was not able to pour silicone into the mold, so I did not have my own chocolate. The biggest challenge was probably making sure that the logo poppued up instead of going in, so that I can form a mold.


Molding A Coaster

This is my finished product!

My last assignment for this rotation was to create a coaster using urethane and expoxy. I poured a mixture of oragne dye and urethane to create the base of my coaster. I chose to put in fine-looking rocks into the coaster, becuase I felt like it would be a good combination of nature with my artificial skills.

What did I learn?

Rotation 6 was probably the busiest week for me, sinse there were so much going on at a short period. My biggest takeaway from this rotation was that it was pretty simple to print out any stickers that I wanted. It was also fascinating to create a mold using urethane and expoxy since they were hands-on rather than creating a CAD design or from the computer. I felt accomplished by the fact that I can now integrate digital materials with pre-made objects.
